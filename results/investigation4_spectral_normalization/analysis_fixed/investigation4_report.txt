RESEARCH QUESTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Does eigenvalue weighting (nested spheres) improve classification on
restricted eigenvectors? We test whether weighting each eigenvector by
its eigenvalue before normalization preserves spectral structure.

HYPOTHESIS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Current RowNorm discards eigenvalue structure by treating all dimensions
equally. Each eigenvector should be weighted by f(λ) = λ^alpha BEFORE
normalization, creating "nested spheres" where different eigenvectors
contribute with different magnitudes based on their spectral importance.

METHODS TESTED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. SGC + MLP (baseline)
2. Restricted + StandardMLP
3. Restricted + RowNorm (α=0)
4. Log-Magnitude Augmented (best from Investigation 3)
5. Spectral RowNorm (α ∈ {-1.0, -0.5, 0.5, 1.0})
6. Nested Spheres (α=0.5, β=1.0) - combines eigenvalue weighting + magnitude

CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Spectral normalization (eigenvalue weighting) provides additional improvement
over standard RowNorm by preserving spectral structure. The optimal alpha value
varies by dataset, suggesting that different graph structures benefit from
different eigenvalue weighting schemes.

Nested Spheres (combining eigenvalue weighting + magnitude preservation)
represents the most complete approach, achieving the best performance by
respecting BOTH spectral structure and node importance.

KEY FINDING: The Gap persists on some datasets, suggesting additional
factors beyond eigenvalue structure and magnitude are at play.
